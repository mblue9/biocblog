Introducing CuratedAtlasQueryR package

CuratedAtlasQueryR is a new package that enables easy programmatic exploration of CELLxGENE single-cell human cell atlas data.

Package
Authors

Stefano Mangiola

Maria Doyle

Published

February 21, 2023

This blog post was co-written by the lead developer of CuratedCellAtlasR, Stefano Mangiola, and the Bioconductor Community Manager, Maria Doyle.

We are excited to announce the launch of the CuratedCellAtlasR package! Millions of cells are now at your fingertips as the harmonized and reannotated CELLxGENE single-cell human atlas can be explored and retrieved as a Bioconductor SingleCellExperiment object.

Why this package was created

The Human Cell Atlas is a large-scale single-cell sequencing initiative that aims to map every cell type in the human body. It has the potential to revolutionise our understanding of human cellular biology and the immune system. Data harmonisation, curation and effective data query are essential to extract knowledge from these complex atlases. The CELLxGENE human cell atlas provides a curated, searchable version of the Human Cell Atlas data. To facilitate programmatic access to this atlas through R, we have developed CuratedAtlasQueryR, a cell-resolved query system that allows researchers to select cells based on their type, tissue of origin, and demographics.

The rationale for our package is that, while CELLxGENE is not harmonised across dataset, we made it so, so you can do cross-dataset analyses.

How it differs from existing packages

We use the existing Bioconductor package, cellxgenedp as download source, and harmonise the data. Differently from cellxgenedp, we can query specific cells across datasets based on annotation, all this without loading into memory. So our query resolution is at the cell level rather than dataset.

The harmonized and reannotated CELLxGENE singlecell human atlas can be explored and retrieved as a Bioconductor SingleCellExperiment object.

We use DuckDB in the package due to it’s fast speed, and low disk imprint in handling large amounts of data.

How you can use it

Step 1: Get started with CuratedCellAtlasR by exploring the integrated metadata of 28 million cells (on-disk) using tidyverse. The atlas includes 344 studies across 40 tissues, providing raw abundances and counts-per-million. 📊

Step 2: Filter cells of interest using tidyverse, whether it’s a specific dataset or a cell type across tissues and diseases. CuratedAtlasQueryR makes it easy to find what you’re looking for! 🔍

Step 3: Collect the SingleCellExperiment for the cells of interest. Our object uses HDF5 format to keep the R session light, making it easier to work with larger datasets. 💻

Step 4: With CuratedAtlasQueryR and tidySingleCellExperiment, checking the transcription abundance of your favourite gene and cell type across diseases, tissues and hundreds of datasets is just a few lines of code! 🎉
